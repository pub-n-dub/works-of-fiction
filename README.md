## Works of Fiction

Done in a previous life and still made available to the public.

### First-line commentary


This repo feels like a serious body of work, not a hobby folder. There’s real substance here: The Black House is already assembled as a 235-page collection in both Word and PDF, Disturbed has been prepared as a 92-page script package, and Ninety-Nine is a full novel-length manuscript with a strong opening voice. The dark, gothic, psychological identity is clear across the material, and the artwork in artwork/the-black-house.jpg and artwork/disturbed.png gives it a distinct visual world.

What holds it back from feeling professionally publishable right now is mostly packaging, positioning, and editorial finish. The repo is structured like a personal archive rather than a publication-ready catalog: fragments, notes, inserted pieces, Kindle build artifacts, old .doc files, and output PDFs are all mixed together. The public-facing framing is especially thin: README.md is one line, and the production metadata still exposes internal history like malfactor, boris, and default PDF titles such as “Microsoft Word - The Black House.docx” in the exported files. That alone would make a professional release feel unfinished.

On the writing side, the strongest signal I got is atmosphere and conviction. The Black House opens with a clear gothic sensibility, and Ninety-Nine has a vivid, lived-in voice. The tradeoff is that both likely need a strong modern editorial pass before publication. Some phrasing, pacing, and sexual material read like work from an earlier era of the manuscripts and may feel dated or overextended to current readers unless deliberately revised. Disturbed is a separate case: it reads like a screenplay, so its professional path is different from a novel or story collection. I would not treat that as a standard book release unless you specifically want a published script edition.

If the goal is professional publication, I’d focus the project instead of trying to publish the whole archive at once. My recommendation would be:

Lead with The Black House as the flagship book.
Put Ninety-Nine through the deepest editorial review before deciding on release.
Treat Disturbed as a screenplay property, not part of the same publishing lane.
Before publishing, I’d want these in place:

One final canonical manuscript per title.
Clean metadata, title pages, copyright pages, author name, imprint, and consistent trim-size interiors.
Proper EPUB and print-ready files, not just Word-to-PDF exports.
Back-cover copy, author bio, keywords/BISAC categories, and a clear brand identity.
A cleaned repo or separate production repo so the professional assets are isolated from drafts and fragments.
My honest take: yes, there’s enough here to publish professionally, but not yet in its current state. The creative core is real; what’s missing is the editorial and production discipline that makes readers, retailers, or presses trust the package.
