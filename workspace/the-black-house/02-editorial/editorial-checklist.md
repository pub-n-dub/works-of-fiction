# Editorial Checklist

## Developmental

- confirm the intended audience and market position
- confirm whether the collection should read as literary horror, gothic fiction, or dark speculative fiction
- review story order for pacing and cumulative effect
- identify any stories that feel stylistically out of step with the collection

## Line Edit

- tighten repetitive phrasing
- modernize any language that reads dated unless intentionally retained
- normalize punctuation, italics, capitalization, and heading style
- verify names, internal references, and timeline details

## Copyedit/Proof

- remove double spaces and inconsistent quotation styling
- verify table of contents pagination after layout
- check copyright page, edition statement, and imprint details
- proof final EPUB and print PDF separately

## Current Risks

- source-of-truth manuscript is not yet formally locked
- public-facing metadata is inconsistent across files
- archival and production materials are mixed together in the source directory
