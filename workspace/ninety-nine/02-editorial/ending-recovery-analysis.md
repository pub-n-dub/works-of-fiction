# Ending Recovery Analysis

## Question

Is there any surviving clue in the nearby `Ninety-Nine` source material
that helps recover the missing ending or final `thirty days` section?

## Short Answer

No direct continuation of the ending was found in the nearby fragment
files.

However, the source set does contain several strong indirect clues about
how the missing ending problem happened and where the remaining material
may once have existed.

## Strongest Findings

### 1. The current manuscript stops abruptly in a dated sequence

The working manuscript runs through a late diary-like progression:

- `May 2`
- `May 3`
- `May 4`
- `May 5`
- `May 6-7`
- `May 8`
- `May 9`
- `May 10`
- `May 11`
- `May 12`

It then stops without narrative closure.

This matters because the manuscript itself appears to be moving day by
day toward a later event horizon:

- moving out from Rhindy
- obtaining money by `May 28`
- relocating on `June 1`
- seeing whether Heather becomes a real escape route or another failure

That structure is consistent with the user memory that a final
`thirty days` section is missing.

### 2. There is an internal marker: `BEGIN KEYING IN REMAINDER OF NOVEL:`

Near the late Heather beach sequence, the manuscript includes this
explicit line:

- `BEGIN KEYING IN REMAINDER OF NOVEL:`

This is the most important recovery clue in the whole file.

It suggests that the latter portion of the book may have been retyped or
re-entered from another source rather than preserved as one continuous
draft. That increases the likelihood that:

- the keyed-in remainder was only partially entered
- the source pages for the remainder once existed elsewhere
- the final segment may have been lost before or during re-entry

### 3. None of the nearby fragment files continue the late Heather/Rhindy sequence

The following files were extracted and checked:

- `Bits and Pieces (99).doc`
- `Bits and Pieces 2 (99).doc`
- `Ninety-Nine--Cuts.doc`
- `The Story With No Middle (fragment).doc`
- `Sex in the 90s.doc`
- `Prague Tales/18 February 2007.doc`
- `Prague Tales/howie0.odt`

Result:

- no `May 13` or later continuation
- no `June 1` follow-through
- no direct continuation of the Heather / Janet / Rhindy late sequence
- no alternate final chapter
- no explicit `thirty days` section

### 4. Some fragments are clearly source-DNA, but not ending recovery

The fragment files do show that older material was reused or absorbed
into the novel:

- `Bits and Pieces (99)` contains early `Ninety-Nine` / Gale affair talk
- `Bits and Pieces 2 (99)` contains Gale material and Rhindy conflict
- `Ninety-Nine--Cuts` is labeled as a Chapter 4 cut

These are useful for editorial genealogy, but they do not recover the
ending.

## Likely Interpretation

The best current reading of the evidence is:

1. `Ninety-Nine.doc` is the best surviving master manuscript.
2. Its ending is incomplete, not merely rough.
3. The missing material was likely meant to continue beyond `May 12`
   through the move-out / breakup / Heather arc.
4. The line `BEGIN KEYING IN REMAINDER OF NOVEL:` strongly suggests that
   some later source pages once existed outside this file.
5. Those source pages are not present in the currently adjacent
   fragments.

## Most Plausible Missing Material Zone

If the final `thirty days` memory is accurate, the missing zone likely
covered some or all of the period between:

- `May 12`
- `May 28` money deadline
- `June 1` planned move
- and the emotional aftermath of leaving or failing to leave Rhindy

That would fit both the dated structure already in the manuscript and
the unresolved pressure built into the late chapters.

## Recommended Next Steps

1. Search outside the current `Ninety-Nine/` folder for loose pages,
   old exports, backups, email attachments, or alternate filenames.
2. Search for material keyed around the phrases:
   - `BEGIN KEYING IN REMAINDER OF NOVEL`
   - `May 12`
   - `May 28`
   - `June 1`
   - `Heather`
   - `Janet`
   - `Rhindy`
3. Treat the current manuscript ending as a recoverable structural gap,
   not as a final chapter.
4. If no further source is found, reconstruct the missing final section
   from the existing timeline and character pressure already present in
   the manuscript.

## Editorial Conclusion

There is no recovered ending in the nearby fragments.

The most useful clue is not a missing page but the manuscript's own
warning sign: `BEGIN KEYING IN REMAINDER OF NOVEL:`. That line makes it
very plausible that the lost ending once existed in another physical or
digital source that was only partially incorporated into the surviving
master file.
